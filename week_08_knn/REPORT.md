# Lab 04 — KNN Classifier Report
## Domain: Fitness Injury Risk Prediction

---

## 1. What domain did you choose and why?

I chose **fitness injury risk prediction** because it has a natural, intuitive
classification boundary (high training load + poor recovery = injury) and
realistic feature scales that make scaling effects easy to observe.

The problem is binary: given a week of training data, predict whether an athlete
is `at_risk` of injury or `safe`.

---

## 2. How was the data generated? How was noise injected?

Three features were sampled from uniform distributions:

| Feature | Range | Real-world unit |
|---|---|---|
| `training_volume_hrs` | 2 – 20 | hours / week |
| `sleep_hours` | 4 – 10 | hours / night |
| `protein_intake_g` | 40 – 200 | grams / day |

A **weighted score** was computed:

```
score = 0.5 × (training_volume − 2)/18
      − 0.3 × (sleep_hours − 4)/6
      − 0.2 × (protein_intake − 40)/160
      + Normal(0, 0.13)          ← noise term
```

Points with `score > 0.2` were labelled `at_risk`; others `safe`.

The `Normal(0, 0.13)` term ensures that the boundary is **not linearly
separable**, matching real-world ambiguity where two athletes with identical
stats may have different outcomes.

---

## 3. What was the optimal K? How was it found?

- **√N rule**: K = √240 ≈ 15.5 → rounded to **K = 15** (nearest odd)
- The **K vs Accuracy** plot (`viz2_k_accuracy_curve.png`) was generated by
  training models for K = 1, 3, 5, …, 49 and recording train and test accuracy.

From the plot:
- K = 1 → train accuracy ≈ 1.00 (clear **overfitting**)
- K = 15–21 → test accuracy peaks (sweet spot, **good generalisation**)
- K > 35 → both curves converge downward (**underfitting**)

The √N heuristic lands in the optimal zone.

---

## 4. Which feature contributed most to classification?

**`training_volume_hrs`** contributes most (weight 0.5 in the score formula).
Evidence from the distribution matrix (`viz3_feature_distribution.png`):

- The histogram for `training_volume_hrs` shows the cleanest separation between
  classes: `at_risk` samples cluster at higher training volumes.
- `sleep_hours` shows moderate separation.
- `protein_intake_g` shows the least separation on its own (lowest weight 0.2).

---

## 5. What happened when feature scaling was removed?

Without `StandardScaler` (see `viz4_before_after_scaling.png`):

- `protein_intake_g` has a range of **~160** while `sleep_hours` has a range of
  **~6** — a 27× difference.
- Euclidean distance is dominated entirely by `protein_intake_g`, making
  `sleep_hours` effectively invisible to the KNN algorithm.
- Decision boundaries become axis-aligned and inaccurate.

After scaling (μ = 0, σ = 1 per feature):
- All three features contribute equally to distance calculations.
- The boundary reflects the true multi-feature logic.
- **Test accuracy improves by ~5–8%** when scaling is applied.

---

## 6. What are the limitations of KNN for this dataset?

1. **Memory-based**: KNN stores all 240 training points and computes distance to
   every one at prediction time — poor scalability for large datasets.
2. **Curse of dimensionality**: With more features, distance becomes less
   meaningful. Adding irrelevant features hurts performance.
3. **Equal feature assumption**: Even after scaling, KNN treats all features as
   equally informative. A weighted distance metric or feature selection would
   help.
4. **No probabilistic model**: KNN's probability estimates are crude
   (vote counts / K). Logistic regression would give better-calibrated
   confidence.
5. **Sensitive to noise**: The ±0.13 Gaussian noise creates overlapping regions
   near the boundary where KNN may misclassify, and there's no way to learn
   around these overlaps.

---

## 7. If you could add a 4th feature, what would it be and why?

I would add **`resting_heart_rate_bpm`** (range ~45–100 bpm).

Elevated resting heart rate is a well-established biomarker for insufficient
recovery and overtraining syndrome. It would:

- Provide orthogonal information to the current features (sleep and training
  volume don't directly encode cardiovascular fatigue).
- Have a scale similar to `protein_intake_g` — reinforcing the importance of
  scaling.
- Increase the model's ability to catch athletes who train lightly but are
  still at risk due to underlying fatigue.
